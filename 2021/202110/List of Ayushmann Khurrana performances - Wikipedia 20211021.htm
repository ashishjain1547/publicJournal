List of Ayushmann Khurrana performances From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia Jump to navigation <#mw-head> Jump to search <#searchInput> Ayushmann Khurrana's filmography Khurrana in 2012 Ayushmann Khurrana is an Indian actor, singer, and television host who works in Hindi films. Khurrana first appeared in 2004 teen drama reality show MTV /Roadies/ , winning the second season of the show.^[1] <#cite_note-1> He went to star in many other MTV shows, including /MTV Fully Faltoo Movies/, /Cheque De India/ and /Jaadoo Ek Baar/, and hosted multiple television shows, including /India's Got Talent / and /Music Ka Maha Muqqabla /.^[2] <#cite_note-:4-2> ^[3] <#cite_note-3> In 2012, he made his feature film debut with the romantic comedy /Vicky Donor /, about sperm donation ,^[4] <#cite_note-:5-4> ^[5] <#cite_note-5> which received critical acclaim and performed strongly at the box office.^[6] <#cite_note-6> ^[7] <#cite_note-7> ^[8] <#cite_note-8> Khurrana's performance won him the Filmfare Award for Best Male Playback Singer (for the song "Pani Da Rang "),^[9] <#cite_note-9> Screen Award for Best Male Debut ,^[10] <#cite_note-10> Producers Guild Film Award for Best Male Debut ,^[11] <#cite_note-:6-11> and Producers Guild Film Award for Best Male Playback Singer .^[11] <#cite_note-:6-11> He then starred in a series of commercially unsuccessful films, including the comedy-drama /Nautanki Saala / (2013),^[12] <#cite_note-12> romantic comedy /Bewakoofiyaan / (2014),^[13] <#cite_note-13> and drama /Hawaizaada / (2015).^[14] <#cite_note-14> In 2015, Khurrana starred in the Sharat Katariya -directed romantic drama /Dum Laga Ke Haisha / opposite Bhumi Pednekar .^[15] <#cite_note-:7-15> His performance was praised,^[16] <#cite_note-16> ^[17] <#cite_note-17> ^[18] <#cite_note-18> and the film emerged as a commercial success.^[19] <#cite_note-19> ^[20] <#cite_note-20> He then starred in /Meri Pyaari Bindu / (2017), /Bareilly Ki Barfi / (2017), and /Shubh Mangal Saavdhan / (2017).^[15] <#cite_note-:7-15> The latter two were commercially successful.^[15] <#cite_note-:7-15> In 2018, he starred in the black comedy /Andhadhun / and the comedy-drama /Badhaai Ho /.^[21] <#cite_note-21> ^[22] <#cite_note-22> The former grossed ₹4.56 billion (US$64 million) worldwide, and became one of Indian cinema's biggest grossers ;^[23] <#cite_note-:8-23> for his performance he won the National Film Award for Best Actor (shared with Vicky Kaushal for /Uri: The Surgical Strike /) and the Filmfare Critics Award for Best Actor .^[24] <#cite_note-24> ^[25] <#cite_note-25> /Badhaai Ho/ became a sleeper hit , earning over ₹2.21 billion (US$31 million) worldwide.^[23] <#cite_note-:8-23> ^[26] <#cite_note-26> This success continued with Khurrana's 2019 releases, /Article 15 /, /Dream Girl /, and /Bala ./^[27] <#cite_note-27> ^[28] <#cite_note-28> For the first of these, he won the Filmfare Critics Award for Best Actor ,^[29] <#cite_note-29> and Screen Award for Best Actor (Critics) .^[30] <#cite_note-30> In the comedy /Bala/,
