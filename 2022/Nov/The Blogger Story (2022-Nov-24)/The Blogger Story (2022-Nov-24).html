Today is 24th Nov, 2022. In six days from today the month the will be over and the survival8's monthly stats stand at: 4922 page views. If things go with the same current flow, the stats might touch 5500. 
The blog had first touched the monthly page views count of 5000 in the month of February 2018 when it had received a footfall of 5114 page views.
The growth from the past 4.75 years has been wiped out. The growth cycle of monthly page views doubling every two years we were seeing till the June month of 2021 is officially over.

The three landmark developments in the life of survival8 could be named as:
1. WILP Club
2. Book Club 
3. Tech Club 

The WILP Club was when we were posting about the BITS WILP question papers, solutions, assignments and quizzes that was from 2017-2018.
Book Club was when we were sharing book summaries. That was 2019 onwards. 
Tech Club was when we started sharing technology related articles from Jun 2020 onwards.

Growth Hacks



1. The Navigation Bar


The first hack came in very early in the survival8's lifetime. When I was creating the blog to post BITS Pilani, WILP (Work Integrated Learning Program) content, the problem I saw in existing blogs was that the navigation was terrible (if it at all was there). One such navigation that was common in WILP related blogs was Word Cloud. Apart from being too random, the Word Cloud is an inappropriate representation for navigation.

My solution was the best and most commonly found navigation tool available across internet: The Navigation Bar.
And not just a navigation but a very clean navigation bar with drop downs and context menus.

Navigation Bar (default view)




Navigation Bar (expanded view)




The Trouble

Except, there was one problem. I was having the navigation in a separate page and I was making an AJAX call to that page to bring the navigation and other header content at the current page instead of copying header code on every page.
The AJAX call to the navigation page meant every page view on any page with the navigation would result in 2 page views.
Hence the doubling of the page views early on.

3. The WhatsApp Advertising


Now there were WhatsApp groups of BITS WILP students of which I was a part. All that I needed to do after creating a blog post was share it's link the 100's of such groups. One for each subject, one for each semester, one for new joiners, one for alumni, groups, all kind of groups.

When this mass advertising was at it's height, I had a reach of roughly 10,000 people through these groups.
