The first month was spent largely on getting my things together.
I was on bench till the mid of August. Then I joined an MVP project. MVP is Most Viable Product.
The MVP was completed on time in the one month's time. My role required me to develop a software component they called as Python Converter, which was part of a bigger ETL tool for Merck.
Due to being an MVP project that was nearing deadline, it required me to work on every Saturday. Not that I didn't get a compensatory off but it left mostly tired and bruised as I was under extreme pressure to hold on to project for at least 6 months before I am on bench again.
6 months was a time I had in mind because September is the last month of the year of appraisal cycle.
Me taking on a project should not have appeared like an eye wash to my managers.
The project ended with the MVP deadline by September mid.
I got on a research project after it and even though the work was as I had hoped for. It required that I spend a lot time researching about things that even my managers didn't fully know about.
